# 到店接待

## 对话 1：有预约，到店入座

**A (顾客):** Hi, we have a reservation for seven.  
**B (前台):** Sure, under what name?  
**A:** Chen, Mark Chen.  
**B:** Got it, Mr. Chen. Table for four. Right this way, please.  
**A:** Thanks.

> **语言点**
> - **We have a reservation for [time]** — 到店报预约的标准开场，简洁直接
> - **under what name?** — 前台万能问句，跟电话里 `I have a reservation under [name]` 对应
> - **Right this way, please** — 服务员引导用语，比 Follow me 更礼貌地道

> **扩展：一次性说完（更自然）**
> **A (顾客):** Hi, I have a reservation for seven under Mark Chen.  
> **B (前台):** Got it, Mr. Chen. Right this way, please.  
> **A:** Thanks.

## 对话 2：没预约，直接来有位置

**A:** Hi, do you have a table for two?  
**B:** Yeah, we've got a few tables open. Indoor or outdoor?  
**A:** Indoor, please.  
**B:** Sure, follow me. Here's your menu. Your server will be right with you.

> **语言点**
> - **Do you have a table for [number]?** — walk-in（无预约直接来）标准问法
> - **we've got a few tables open** — open 表示"空着的"，不是"营业中"
> - **Your server will be right with you** — 服务员马上来的标准说法，right with you = 立刻就来

## 对话 3：没预约，需要等位

**A:** Hi, table for three, please.  
**B:** We're pretty busy tonight. There'll be about a twenty-minute wait.  
**A:** That's fine.  
**B:** Can I get your name? I'll text you when the table's ready.  
**A:** Sure, it's Lin, Sarah Lin.  
**B:** All right, Sarah. I'll put you on the list.

> **语言点**
> - **There'll be about a [number]-minute wait** — 等位时间的标准说法。也可以说 It's a twenty-minute wait
> - **That's fine** — 接受等位，简洁。不想等可以说 That's too long, we'll come back later
> - **I'll put you on the list** — 把你加到等候名单上
> - **I'll text you when the table's ready** — 现在餐厅普遍做法，排到位了发短信通知

## 对话 4：等位时问进度

**A:** Hi, we've been waiting for about twenty minutes. How much longer?  
**B:** You're next. There's one party ahead of you.  
**A:** Okay, thanks.

> **语言点**
> - **How much longer?** — 万能追问，不问具体时间也能催
> - **You're next** — 就轮到你了
> - **There's one party ahead of you** — 前面还有一组。party 在这里指"一桌客人"，不是"派对"

## 对话 5：到店后要求换桌

**A:** Hi, we're at the table by the window. Is it possible to move to a quieter spot?  
**B:** Let me check if we have anything available... Yeah, I've got a booth in the back. Let me move you over.  
**A:** Thanks, that's much better.

> **语言点**
> - **Is it possible to move to...** — 委婉要求换桌
> - **a booth in the back** — 里面的卡座，通常更安静
> - **Let me move you over** — 我帮你们换过去

## 对话 6：到店发现预约信息有误

**A:** Hi, I have a reservation under Amy Liu at seven-thirty for three.  
**B:** Hmm, I'm not seeing that in our system. Are you sure it was confirmed?  
**A:** Yeah, I booked it online. Let me show you the confirmation email.  
**B:** Oh, there it is. Looks like it was under "Amy L." Sorry about that. Let me get your table.  
**A:** No problem, thanks.

> **语言点**
> - **I'm not seeing that in our system** — 委婉表达"查不到"，比直接说 It doesn't exist 更客气
> - **Are you sure it was confirmed?** — 委婉质疑，不是直接否定
> - **Sorry about that** — 服务方道歉的标准用语，后面通常紧跟解决方案

## 对话 7：到店太早 / 还没准备好

**A:** Hi, I have a reservation for eight. I know we're a bit early.  
**B:** That's no problem. We can seat you now if you'd like, or you can wait at the bar.  
**A:** We'll just wait outside for a bit.  
**B:** Sure, come back around seven-fifty and we'll have your table ready.

> **语言点**
> - **I know we're a bit early** — 到店太早时的礼貌说法
> - **if you'd like** — 委婉给选项，不是强制
> - **We'll have your table ready** — 到时候桌子会准备好，承诺式表达
